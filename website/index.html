Our Mission Meet the Team

In Collaboration With

ICOR Technology

ICOR Technology is an Ottawa-based company specializing in the design and in-house manufacturing of advanced robots and security equipment for EOD (Explosive Ordnance Disposal), SWAT, HAZMAT, and other tactical/military operations.

Since its founding in 2005, ICOR has produced its full line of CALIBER® robots and related equipment entirely within its 27,500 sq ft facility in Ottawa, from R&D and prototyping to final assembly and quality assurance. Their products are used globally by law-enforcement, military, and first-responder agencies in over 60 countries, reflecting ICOR’s reputation for reliability, innovation, and mission-critical safety solutions.

Their commitment to engineering excellence, safety, and service makes ICOR a trusted partner for clients requiring robust electromechanical systems which aligns closely with the skills and training of our Electromechanical Engineering Technician program.

About our Program

School of Advanced Technology (SAT) - Algonquin College

Electromechanical Engineering Technician

What We Learn

Our curriculum blends theory with lab-based projects that mirror real industrial environments. Students develop competencies in:

  • Electrical & Electronic Circuits (DC/AC)
  • Pneumatics & Motor Controls
  • Microcontrollers & Embedded Systems
  • Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC)
  • CAD/CAM Design & Manufacturing
  • Industrial Robotics & Mechatronics
  • Quality Assurance & Project Management

How We Learn

The program emphasizes learning by doing. Students spend much of their time in labs working with real equipment — PLCs, industrial robots, sensors, and automated cells.

Collaborative projects encourage teamwork, communication, and problem-solving — skills that are essential in modern engineering environments.

Co-op & Industry

Through the co-op option, students gain paid work experience in real industrial settings, applying classroom knowledge to real-world challenges.

This experience bridges the gap between academic learning and professional practice, helping students enter the workforce ready to contribute from day one.

Career Pathways

Graduates are well prepared for positions such as:

  • Automation Technician / PLC Programmer
  • Maintenance or Mechatronic Technician
  • Robotics Operator / Integrator
  • Quality Assurance or Process Technician
  • Field Service or Manufacturing Technician

In Short

Our program produces versatile professionals who can bridge the gap between mechanical systems and digital control — a combination that drives innovation in today’s automated world.
