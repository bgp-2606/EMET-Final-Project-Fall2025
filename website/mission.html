Our Mission

Proof of Concept for ICOR Technology

We are developing a fully automated CNC lathe production cell as a proof of concept for our client, ICOR Technology. The primary goal is to reduce inventory costs by transitioning from batch manufacturing to on-demand part production. This system also aims to significantly reduce Quality Control (QC) costs and minimize human intervention, ensuring consistent quality and efficiency.

Process Flow

From Raw Material to Verified Part

Feed Material

Stepper motor feeds bar stock into position.

Secure & Simulate

Pneumatic chuck clamps; machining is simulated.

Robot Transfer

Fanuc arm moves part to inspection station.

Scan & Verify

3D scan compares part to CAD file.

Project Subsystems

Engineered for Precision and Automation

Feeder Subsystem

Feeds the raw material bar into the chuck with precision.

  • Sensors: Two sensors (pre-chuck & post-chuck) ensure accurate positioning.
  • Actuation: Stepper motor controlled by an Arduino Nano.
  • Fixture: Custom-designed fixtures for the motor and ball rollers to support the bar.

Pneumatic Chuck

Secures the material for the simulated machining process.

  • Design: Custom aluminum frame holding 3 pneumatic cylinders spaced 120Â° apart.
  • Inserts: Custom-designed rod tip inserts to accommodate various material sizes.
  • Simulation: Cylinders extend to clamp, simulating the machining hold.

Laser Scanner

Performs Quality Assurance (QA) by generating a 3D model.

  • Hardware: Line scanner, Raspberry Pi camera, and a rotating base (stepper motor).
  • Process: Part rotates while laser scans; camera captures geometry.
  • Analysis: Generates a .obj file and compares it to the reference CAD.
  • Accuracy: Achieves a tolerance of 1.25 mm.

System Integration

Orchestrating the Automation

These three subsystems work in harmony, coordinated by a Fanuc 200iD Robot Arm for material handling and an Omron CJ2M PLC as the master controller. A Raspberry Pi 4 handles the complex image processing and data analysis for the QA process.
